About

Resilience and Refuge Research Services is a research consulting group focused on the intersections of climate change, displacement, and mobility. We collaborate with NGOs, researchers, and policymakers to generate rigorous, grounded insights that support more just and effective responses to climate-related migration and forced displacement.

Our work bridges qualitative and quantitative methods, centering the lived experiences of affected communities while remaining accountable to partnersâ€™ practical needs and timelines.

Projects

Climate-Induced Displacement Mapping

A mixed-methods project examining how slow-onset climate impacts (drought, sea-level rise, ecosystem degradation) shape patterns of mobility and immobility. We work with partners to co-develop indicators, visualize spatial data, and contextualize quantitative trends with qualitative insights.

Urban Refuge & Informal Settlements

Qualitative research with displaced communities in urban and peri-urban settings, focusing on housing precarity, access to services, and everyday strategies of resilience. Outputs include policy briefs, workshop facilitation, and co-authored reports with local organizations.

Collaborative Research Design Support

Advisory support for research teams designing projects on climate, migration, and displacement. This can include refining research questions, methods selection, ethical protocols, and participatory approaches that meaningfully involve affected communities.
